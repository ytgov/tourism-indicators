Highlights
  • 2024 is on track to be a record year for sales at restaurants and bars in the Yukon, with a 8.2% increase year over year from January to October 2024.
  • Sales rebounded strongly post-2020, with YTD sales rising from $70.3M in 2020 to $103.9M in 2023, surpassing the pre-pandemic high of $83.9M in 2019.
  • The YTD sales shows an overall upward trend, growing significantly from $45.6M in 2000 to $103.9M in 2023. This indicates sustained growth in restaurant and bar sales over the years.

This dashboard presents insights into monthly restaurant and bar sales in the Yukon, based on data from the Monthly Survey of Food Services and Drinking Places. The survey tracks the sales of restaurants, caterers, and drinking establishments classified under NAICS 722, providing vital information for federal and territorial governments, industry associations, and businesses for planning, marketing, and policy-making. Updates typically occur within 3 months.

Data Source: Statistics Canada, Monthly Survey of Food Services and Drinking Places. Table 21-10-0019-01.
