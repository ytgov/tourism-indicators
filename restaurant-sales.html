Highlights
  • 2024 is on track to be a record year for restaurant and bar sales in Yukon, with an 8.2% increase from January to October compared to last year.
  • Sales have grown since 2020, rising from $70.3 million in 2020 to $103.9 million in 2023, beating the pre-pandemic high of $83.9 million in 2019.
  • Year-to-date sales have been going up over time, growing from $45.6 million in 2000 to $103.9 million in 2023, showing steady growth in the food and drink industry.
  • Higher sales do not always mean higher profits, as rising costs for food, labour, and supplies have also impacted businesses.

Notes on data: Numbers are not adjusted for inflation. This dashboard presents insights into monthly restaurant and bar sales in the Yukon, based on data from the Monthly Survey of Food Services and Drinking Places. The survey tracks the sales of restaurants, caterers, and drinking establishments classified under NAICS 722, providing vital information for federal and territorial governments, industry associations, and businesses for planning, marketing, and policy-making. Updates typically occur within 3 months.

Source: Statistics Canada. Table 21-10-0019-01 Monthly survey of food services and drinking places (x 1,000).
