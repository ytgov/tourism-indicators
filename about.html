About the Yukon Tourism Economic Dashboard

Purpose

This interactive dashboard provides a comprehensive view of Yukon's tourism economic landscape, offering insights into the sector's contribution to the regional economy. Our goal is to transparently present data that helps stakeholders, policymakers, and researchers understand the economic impact of tourism in Yukon.

Data Sources

The dashboard integrates data from multiple authoritative sources:

  • Yukon Business Survey, (2009-2023)
  • Statistics Canada
  • Tourism Data Collective

    • Transparency

    We are committed to providing clear, accessible economic information. All data sources are cited, and methodological limitations are openly disclosed.

    Contact

    For more information or data inquiries, please contact tourismresearch@yukon.ca.

    Glossary of Terms

    Metric Definition Page
    Visitors The number of people that have travelled 40km or more and either stayed overnight (overnight visitor) or returned home that day (same day visitors). Visitor Estimates
    Air Arrivals The number of people who disembark from an international or domestic flight at Erik Nielsen Whitehorse International Airport. Air Arrivals
    Revenue Attributed to Tourism The total economic value generated by tourism activities within a specific region or sector, including direct spending by tourists, indirect economic impacts on related industries, and induced economic effects from tourism-related income. Economic Overview
    GDP Attributed to Tourism The percentage of a region's GDP directly or indirectly linked to tourism activities. This includes direct tourism business revenues, indirect economic benefits from tourism-supporting industries, and economic multiplier effects of tourism spending. Economic Overview
    Gross Revenues The total income generated by a business or sector before subtracting expenses, taxes, or other deductions. Economic Overview
    Perceived GDP Attribution An estimate of economic contribution based on business owners' perceptions of how much of their revenue comes from a specific sector. This method relies on respondents' "best guess" and may include subjective interpretations. Economic Overview
    Occupancy Rate The percentage of available rooms sold within a specified time. (Demand/Supply) * 100 Hotel PerformanceShort Term Rental Performance
    Average Daily Rate (ADR) A key hospitality metric representing the average revenue earned for a sold room per day. Hotel PerformanceShort Term Rental Performance
    Revenue Per Available Room (RevPAR) A performance metric combining occupancy and room rate to show a hotel's ability to fill its available rooms at an average rate. Calculation: Total Room Revenue ÷ Total Available Rooms OR ADR × Occupancy Rate. Hotel PerformanceShort Term Rental Performance
    Short Term Rental (STR) A property intended for shorter stays, often less than 30 days. Interchangeable with 'vacation rental'. Short Term Rental Performance
    Year-To-Date (YTD) The sum or average of a specific metric from the start of the year to the latest month that data exists.
    Year-over-year (y/y, YoY) Measures the rate of change in a metric accross two comparable periods.
