Highlights

In recent years, international border crossing trends into the Yukon have changed, reflecting shifts in visitor behavior:

  • Crossings at Beaver Creek have slightly increased in recent years but remain lower than their all-time high in 1992.
  • Crossings at Pleasant Camp hit a record low in 2013 but have been steadily growing since.
  • Crossings at Fraser have surged due to more cruise ship passengers docking in Skagway, leading to a rise in day trips from Skagway.

Visitor behavior and transportation choices have also evolved:

  • Train passenger numbers have stayed steady, while bus travel has grown significantly.
  • Most visitors are American, but the number of overseas travelers is increasing.
  • Visitor numbers from Europe have stayed the same or declined, while more travelers are coming from Asia, Oceania, and the Americas.
