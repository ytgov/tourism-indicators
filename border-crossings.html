Highlights

In recent years, there has been a notable shift in the trends of international border crossings into Yukon, highlighting changing visitor dynamics:

  • While crossings at Beaver Creek have experienced a slight resurgence in recent years, the overall trend has been downward since reaching an all-time high in 1992.
  • Crossings at Pleasant Camp hit a record low in 2013 but have seen steady growth since then.
  • Crossings at Fraser have increased dramatically, driven by the rise in cruise ship passengers docking in Skagway. This has led to a substantial increase in day trips from Skagway, although overnight visitation is trending downward.

Visitor behavior and modes of transport have also evolved:

  • Train passenger numbers have remained steady, while there has been significant growth in bus passengers.
  • The majority of visitors continue to be Americans, but the proportion of overseas visitors is growing.
  • Visitors from Europe have remained stable or are decreasing, while travelers from Asia, Oceania, and the Americas are on the rise.
